## 2. What Personal Data We Collect

We collect the minimum data necessary to provide our Services. The data we collect falls into the following categories.

### 2.1 Data You Provide Directly

- **Account & identity data:** First name, last name, email address, phone number (optional), and profile photo. If you sign in with Apple or Google, we receive only your name and verified email address — we do not access your contacts, calendars, or social media.
- **Workout data:** Exercise selections, sets, reps, weights, session duration, activity type, and any notes you add to your training log.
- **Voice input:** If you use voice logging, your speech is transcribed on-device using Apple Speech. The resulting text transcript — not audio — is sent to our servers for exercise extraction.
- **Atlas AI conversations:** The messages you exchange with Atlas, our AI coaching system, and the topics discussed.
- **Media uploads:** Photos or videos you upload for form checks or Bonfire community posts.
- **Body metrics:** Any body composition data you choose to log (weight, measurements).
- **Preferences & settings:** Unit preferences, notification settings, timezone, and personalization consent.

### 2.2 Data Generated by Your Use of the Platform

- **Training insights:** Analytics derived from your workout history, including personal records, volume trends, consistency scores, and training patterns.
- **$ISO economy data:** Your in-app currency balance, earning history, spending history, streak data, and reward redemptions.
- **Usage data:** Login events, features accessed, session duration, and app heartbeat signals (used to confirm the app is installed).
- **Technical data:** IP address, device type, operating system version, and timezone. Used for security and service delivery.

### 2.3 Data from Connected Platforms (ISO Sync Add-On)

If you subscribe to ISO Sync ($3/mo) and connect external platforms, we receive:

- **Apple Health (via HealthKit):** Workouts, steps, heart rate, sleep, body metrics, VO2 max.
- **Strava (via OAuth2):** Activities, routes, heart rate zones, power, training load.
- **Fitbit (via OAuth2):** Activities, steps, heart rate, sleep stages, SpO2, stress.
- **Garmin (via Push API):** Activities, sleep, body composition, stress, pulse ox.

You control which platforms are connected. You can disconnect at any time from your profile settings. Connection requires explicit authorization through each platform's own consent flow.

### 2.4 Data We Do NOT Collect

We do not collect or store:

- Your location or GPS data (we use timezone only, for scheduling).
- Your contacts, calendar, or social media data.
- Raw audio from voice logging — only text transcripts are transmitted.
- Health data from Apple Health or other platforms unless you explicitly enable ISO Sync and grant permission.

## 3. How & Why We Use Your Data

We process your data only where we have a valid purpose.

- **Provide workout logging and analytics:** Your workout data powers your personal training history, charts, personal records, and training insights.
- **Deliver Atlas AI coaching:** Your training data, profile, and conversation history are used to generate personalized coaching responses (see Section 5 for full detail).
- **Operate the $ISO economy:** Earning, spending, streak tracking, and reward redemption require processing your activity and account data.
- **Facilitate community features (Bonfire):** If you post to The Bonfire, your content, nickname, and tier are visible to other members. Free-tier users can view but not post or interact.
- **Process payments:** Subscription management and billing through Stripe.
- **Synthesize cross-platform data (ISO Sync):** If subscribed, we process connected platform data to generate a unified training analysis.
- **Platform security:** Technical data and audit logs are used for fraud prevention and service reliability.
- **Communicate with you:** Account notifications, operational alerts, and support correspondence via email.

## 4. Consent & Personalization

Atlas AI personalization is opt-in. On first interaction with Atlas, you are asked:

> "Make Atlas smarter — Allow Atlas to remember what you discuss — your goals, your progress, what's working — so every conversation picks up where the last one left off. Your data stays private to ISOfit and is never sold."

If you consent, Atlas builds persistent memory items from your conversations (goals, injuries, preferences). If you decline, Atlas still works — it just doesn't retain context between sessions. You can change this setting at any time from your profile.

Your consent status is stored in a dedicated consent record created automatically when your account is set up. All consent flags default to off.

## 5. Personal Data Shared with Atlas AI

This section describes how your personal data interacts with the Atlas AI system.

### 5.1 What Atlas Receives About You

When you interact with Atlas, the following data is assembled to generate each response:

- **Atlas system prompt:** The coaching identity and behavioral rules (not your data).
- **Curated knowledge base:** Training principles sourced from 12 expert coaches — system-level content, not personal data.
- **Your profile and settings:** Name, experience level, unit preferences.
- **Your training insights:** Aggregated analytics from your workout history (volume trends, consistency, weak points).
- **Your last 30 days of workout logs:** Structured training data, most recent first.
- **Your last 10 Atlas messages:** Conversation continuity within the current thread.
- **Your current message:** The text (and any attached image/video) you just sent.
- **Your persistent memory** (if consent granted): Goals, injuries, preferences Atlas has recorded from past conversations.

### 5.2 How Atlas Uses Your Data to Generate Responses

Atlas operates on the following logic:

- **Context assembly:** Your profile, training data, and conversation history are compiled into a structured prompt sent to the AI model for each message. This data is processed in real-time to generate a coaching response.
- **Model routing:** Messages are routed to the appropriate AI model based on your tier and the type of request (standard coaching vs. deep analysis like form checks or program generation). No personal data is used to select the model — only the request type and your subscription tier.
- **Memory extraction:** If you have granted personalization consent and have exchanged more than 5 messages in a thread, Atlas may asynchronously extract persistent facts (goals, injuries, preferences) to use in future sessions.

### 5.3 AI Model Training

Your conversations with Atlas are NOT used to train any AI models. We use OpenAI's paid API services, which contractually prohibit using customer data for model training. Your messages are sent to OpenAI solely to generate a response and are subject to OpenAI's data retention policy for abuse monitoring (retained up to 30 days, then deleted).

Similarly, if ISO Sync features use AI processing, the same protections apply — your data is processed to generate your personal report and is not used to train models.

### 5.4 Automated Decision-Making

Atlas does not make automated decisions that produce legal or significant effects on you. Atlas provides coaching suggestions — it does not diagnose medical conditions, prescribe treatments, or make binding decisions about your account, access, or membership. Atlas is not a medical professional and does not replace professional medical advice.

### 5.5 Withdrawing Consent for AI Personalization

You may withdraw your consent for Atlas personalization at any time through your profile settings. Withdrawal means:

- Atlas will no longer retain memory items from your conversations.
- Existing memory items will be deleted.
- Atlas will still function — it simply won't remember context between sessions.

Withdrawal does not affect processing that has already taken place.

## 6. Who We Share Your Data With

### 6.1 AI Processing Providers

Your Atlas messages and training context are sent to OpenAI's API to generate coaching responses. OpenAI processes this data under a Data Processing Agreement. OpenAI does not use paid API data to train its models. Data is retained by OpenAI for up to 30 days for abuse monitoring, then deleted.

If playlist recommendations use a separate AI model (Google Gemini), only the specific request data is sent — not your full profile or training history. The same no-training-use protections apply.

### 6.2 Payment Processing

Stripe handles all payment transactions. Your payment card details are processed directly by Stripe and never touch ISOfit servers. Stripe is PCI-DSS Level 1 certified. We store only your Stripe customer ID and subscription status — never card numbers.

### 6.3 Connected Platforms (ISO Sync)

When you connect Apple Health, Strava, Fitbit, or Garmin through ISO Sync, data flows from those platforms to ISOfit. We do not send your ISOfit data back to those platforms. Each platform connection is governed by that platform's own privacy policy in addition to ours. OAuth tokens for Strava, Fitbit, and Garmin are stored encrypted on our servers.

### 6.4 Email Communications

We use Resend for transactional and operational email delivery (account notifications, alerts). Your email address is shared with Resend solely for message delivery.

### 6.5 Community (Bonfire)

If you post to The Bonfire, your content (text, photos, videos), Bonfire nickname, membership tier, and pinned track (if music connected) are visible to other ISOfit members. You control what you post. Your legal name is not displayed — Bonfire uses nicknames.

### 6.6 Leaderboards

If your data appears on ISO POW leaderboards (longest streak, longest active account, $ISO rankings), it is displayed by nickname only. No real names, no total member counts, no percentile rankings.

### 6.7 We Do Not Sell Your Data

ISOfit does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or training data to any third party. We do not use your data for advertising. We do not allow third-party advertisers to access your data. We do not use your training data to train public AI models.

## 7. Data Retention

We retain your data only for as long as necessary to provide the service or as required by law.

- **Account & profile data:** Retained for the duration of your active account. Deleted upon account closure.
- **Workout logs & training history:** Retained for the duration of your active account. Deleted upon account closure.
- **Atlas conversation history:** Retained for the duration of your active account. Deleted upon account closure.
- **Atlas memory items:** Retained while personalization consent is active. Deleted when consent is withdrawn or account is closed.
- **$ISO ledger & economy data:** Retained for the duration of your active account. All $ISO burns on membership cancellation; ledger history deleted on account closure.
- **Bonfire posts & media:** Retained while active. Posts may be archived or deleted by moderation action. All content deleted on account closure.
- **ISO Sync connected platform data:** Retained while ISO Sync subscription is active. On cancellation, data is retained for 90 days, then permanently purged by automated cleanup.
- **Payment & transaction records:** Retained for 7 years as required for financial and tax compliance.
- **Security & audit logs:** 12 months active, then archived. Archived logs retained for 24 months.
- **Technical data (IP, device info):** Retained for 90 days for security purposes, then purged.
- **Deleted accounts:** Upon account deletion, personal data is removed within 30 days. Anonymized, aggregated analytics (e.g., total platform activity counts) may be retained indefinitely as they cannot be traced to any individual.

## 8. Data Security

We implement technical and organizational measures to protect your data:

- **Encryption in transit:** All data transmitted between your device and our servers is encrypted using TLS.
- **Encryption at rest:** Database storage is encrypted. OAuth tokens for connected platforms (Spotify, Strava, Fitbit, Garmin) are encrypted at the application level before database storage.
- **Row-Level Security (RLS):** Your data in our database is isolated at the row level. Database queries enforce that you can only access your own records. Atlas conversation data, workout logs, and economy data are all protected by per-user row-level security policies.
- **Authentication:** Secure authentication via Supabase Auth supporting Sign in with Apple, Google, and email/password. All privileged operations require authenticated sessions. The service-level database key never appears in the client app.
- **Admin access controls:** Administrative access is managed through a dedicated permissions system. There are no implicit admin roles or hidden privileges — all admin access is explicit and auditable.
- **No client-side secrets:** The iOS app contains no service keys, API secrets, or privileged credentials. All sensitive operations go through authenticated server-side functions.

## 10. Your Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information. To exercise any right, contact privacy@isofit.app.

- **Access:** Request a copy of all personal data we hold about you, including workout data, Atlas conversations, and economy history.
- **Correction:** Request correction of any inaccurate data. You can also edit your profile directly in the app.
- **Deletion:** Request deletion of your personal data. You can delete your account from your profile settings. Note: workout logs and Atlas history are permanently deleted — this cannot be undone.
- **Portability:** Request your personal data in a structured, machine-readable format.
- **Withdraw consent:** Withdraw consent for Atlas personalization or any other optional data processing at any time through your profile settings.
- **Opt out of sale:** We do not sell your data. There is nothing to opt out of.

California residents have additional rights under the CCPA, including the right to know what personal information is collected, the right to request deletion, and the right to non-discrimination for exercising privacy rights.

EU residents have rights under the GDPR including the right to erasure, data portability, restriction of processing, and the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority.
